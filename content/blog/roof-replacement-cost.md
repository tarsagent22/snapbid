# Roof Replacement Cost in 2026: What Homeowners Actually Pay

Your roof is leaking, aging out, or getting flagged by your insurance company. Now you need to figure out what a replacement actually costs — before a roofing contractor shows up and hands you a number you can't evaluate.

The national average roof replacement in 2026 costs between **$8,000 and $15,000** for a standard asphalt shingle roof on a 2,000 sqft home. But that range is wide for a reason — material, size, pitch, and your location can push costs significantly in either direction.

Here's exactly how to think about it.

## Roof Replacement Cost by Material

### Asphalt Shingles: $5,000–$12,000

Asphalt is installed on roughly 80% of American homes, and for good reason — it's durable, widely available, and cost-effective.

- **3-tab shingles**: $5,000–$8,000 for an average home (20–25 year lifespan)
- **Architectural/dimensional shingles**: $7,000–$12,000 (25–30 year lifespan, better curb appeal)
- **Premium asphalt (impact-resistant, Class 4)**: $9,000–$14,000 (may lower insurance premiums)

Most homeowners in the mid-range go with architectural shingles — the upgrade from 3-tab is usually only $1,000–$2,000 and gives you a significantly better-looking and longer-lasting roof.

### Metal Roofing: $10,000–$20,000

Metal has had a major resurgence for residential use. It lasts 40–70 years, handles severe weather better than asphalt, and is increasingly competitive on price.

- **Standing seam metal**: $14,000–$20,000 (premium, concealed fasteners)
- **Metal shingles/tiles**: $10,000–$17,000
- **Corrugated/ribbed metal panels**: $10,000–$15,000

Metal costs more upfront but the lifecycle cost often works out lower. It's a strong option if you're planning to stay in the home long-term.

### Tile Roofing: $15,000–$30,000

Clay or concrete tile is common in the Southwest and Southeast. It's extremely durable (50+ years) and adds significant architectural appeal, but it's heavy — older homes may need structural reinforcement first.

- **Concrete tile**: $15,000–$22,000
- **Clay tile**: $20,000–$30,000+

If you're in a HOA that requires tile, budget accordingly. Also factor in that tile repair is specialized work — not every roofer handles it.

## What Actually Determines Your Final Price

### Square Footage: The Biggest Factor

Roofing is priced per "square" — 100 square feet. A 1,500 sqft home typically has 15–18 squares of roof surface (accounting for pitch and overhangs).

- 1,000–1,500 sqft home: $5,500–$10,000 (asphalt)
- 1,500–2,500 sqft home: $8,000–$15,000 (asphalt)
- 2,500–3,500 sqft home: $12,000–$20,000 (asphalt)

### Roof Pitch (Steepness)

A low-slope roof (4:12 pitch or less) is faster and easier to work on. Steep roofs (8:12 or higher) require additional safety equipment and take longer — add 20–40% to labor costs for steep pitches.

### Removing the Old Roof

Most replacements involve tearing off the existing shingles. Some contractors will shingle-over (lay new over old), but building codes in most states allow only two layers max, and a proper tearoff gives you the chance to inspect and repair decking.

- Tearoff labor: $1–$2 per sqft (typically $1,500–$3,500 for an average home)
- Decking repair/replacement: $50–$75 per sheet of plywood (budget for surprises)

### Your Location

Labor costs vary dramatically by region. The same roof job in Dallas might cost $8,500 while the same job in Boston or Seattle runs $13,000. Always get local bids.

> **Pro Tip:** After any major hail storm, your area will get flooded with out-of-state "storm chasers" offering cheap quotes and upfront payment. Stick with locally licensed, insured contractors — check their license with your state contractor board and verify they have a local address. Storm chaser scams cost homeowners hundreds of millions annually.

## What's Included (and What's Not)

A standard roof replacement quote should include:
- Tearoff and disposal of old materials
- New underlayment (felt or synthetic)
- Ice and water shield in critical areas
- Shingles or chosen material
- New flashing around chimneys, vents, and skylights
- Ridge cap and ventilation

**Often not included:**
- Decking repair (priced separately per damaged sheet)
- Gutters (if damaged or mismatched)
- Skylight replacement
- Chimney repointing

Always ask for an itemized bid so you can compare apples to apples.

## Signs You Need a Full Replacement (Not Just Repairs)

- Shingles are curling, cracking, or missing in multiple areas
- Roof is 20+ years old (asphalt) or 15+ (3-tab)
- Granules filling your gutters
- Daylight visible in the attic
- Multiple repairs in the past 3 years
- Insurance adjuster recommends replacement after a storm

A patch job on an aging roof often costs more in the long run than a clean replacement.

## How to Evaluate Roofing Bids

Get at least 3 bids. Look for:

1. Itemized pricing (materials, labor, tearoff, disposal)
2. Specific shingle brand and model (not just "30-year architectural")
3. Manufacturer warranty AND workmanship warranty
4. Proof of insurance (liability + workers' comp)
5. Local references you can actually call

A legitimate roofing contractor won't pressure you to sign the same day.
