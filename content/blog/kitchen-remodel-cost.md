# How Much Does a Kitchen Remodel Cost? (2026 Complete Guide)

If you've been Googling "how much does a kitchen remodel cost," you already know you'll find wildly different numbers depending on where you look. Here's the real answer: it depends heavily on your kitchen size, material choices, and how much of the layout you're changing.

The national average kitchen remodel in 2026 runs between **$25,000 and $60,000** for a mid-range project. But costs can go as low as $10,000 for a cosmetic refresh or north of $150,000 for a full custom gut.

Let's break it all down.

## Kitchen Remodel Cost by Scope

### Minor Remodel: $10,000–$25,000

A minor remodel keeps the bones of your kitchen intact — same layout, same plumbing locations, same electrical. You're refreshing, not rebuilding. This typically includes:

- Replacing cabinet doors and hardware (refacing vs. full replacement)
- New countertops (laminate or entry-level quartz)
- Updated faucet and sink
- New appliances (mid-range)
- Fresh paint and lighting fixtures

This is the sweet spot for kitchens that are functionally fine but look dated.

### Mid-Range Remodel: $25,000–$60,000

This is where most homeowners land. You're getting semi-custom cabinets, stone countertops, and a cohesive design. Common upgrades at this level:

- Semi-custom cabinetry with soft-close drawers
- Quartz or granite countertops
- Tile backsplash
- New appliances (stainless steel, name-brand)
- Recessed lighting and under-cabinet lighting
- Possible minor layout changes

### Major/Luxury Remodel: $60,000–$150,000+

A full gut-and-rebuild with custom cabinets, high-end appliances, and significant structural or layout changes. Think moving walls, adding an island, or opening the kitchen to a living space. At this level you're also paying for top-tier finishes — marble counters, custom range hoods, built-in refrigerators.

## What Actually Drives Kitchen Remodel Costs

### Cabinets: 30–40% of Your Budget

Cabinets are almost always the biggest line item. Here's what to expect:

- **Stock cabinets** (IKEA, big-box): $100–$300 per linear foot installed
- **Semi-custom**: $300–$600 per linear foot
- **Custom**: $500–$1,200+ per linear foot

For a 10x12 kitchen with 20 linear feet of cabinetry, that's $2,000–$24,000 just for cabinets. Choose wisely — this is where splurging or saving has the biggest impact.

### Countertops: $40–$200 per Square Foot

Material makes a massive difference:

- Laminate: $40–$60/sqft installed
- Quartz: $75–$150/sqft installed
- Granite: $65–$130/sqft installed
- Marble: $100–$200/sqft installed

A typical 30 sqft of countertop runs $2,000–$6,000 at mid-range.

### Appliances: $2,000–$20,000+

A full appliance package (refrigerator, range, dishwasher, microwave) from a mid-range brand like Samsung or LG runs $4,000–$8,000. Go with Sub-Zero and Wolf and you're looking at $15,000–$25,000+ just for appliances.

### Labor: 20–35% of Total Budget

General contractor markup, plumber, electrician, tile setter, painter — labor adds up. In high cost-of-living metros (NYC, SF, Boston), add 25–40% to these estimates.

## Hidden Costs Homeowners Miss

- **Permits**: $500–$2,500 depending on scope
- **Temporary kitchen setup**: $200–$500/month (meals out, mini fridge, hot plate)
- **Asbestos or lead paint abatement** (older homes): $1,500–$5,000
- **Structural surprises**: Budget 10–15% contingency

> **Pro Tip:** Get at least 3 bids before signing anything. Contractor quotes for the same kitchen remodel can vary by 30–50%. Knowing the market rate before you start negotiating puts you in a much stronger position.

## How to Keep Costs Under Control

1. **Keep the layout**: Moving the sink or range means moving plumbing or gas lines — add $1,000–$5,000 instantly.
2. **Reface instead of replace**: If your cabinet boxes are solid, refacing can achieve 80% of the look at 30% of the cost.
3. **Shop appliances during sales**: Black Friday and holiday weekends regularly cut 20–30% off appliance packages.
4. **Do your own demo**: A weekend with a sledgehammer and a dumpster rental can save $500–$1,500 in labor.

## How Long Does a Kitchen Remodel Take?

- Minor remodel: 1–3 weeks
- Mid-range: 3–8 weeks
- Major gut/rebuild: 8–16 weeks

Timeline slips are common. Order materials and appliances before demo day — supply chain delays remain a real issue in 2026.

## Is a Kitchen Remodel Worth It?

A mid-range kitchen remodel recoups roughly **70–80% of its cost** at resale according to Remodeling Magazine's 2026 Cost vs. Value Report. A major remodel recoups closer to 50–60%. The best ROI usually comes from mid-range projects that bring the kitchen up to neighborhood standards without over-improving.

But for most homeowners, the daily quality-of-life improvement is the real payoff.
