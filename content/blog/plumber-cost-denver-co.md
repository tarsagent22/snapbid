Denver's rapid growth has pushed demand for skilled tradespeople to an all-time high. Plumbers in particular are in short supply across the Denver metro — from the tech corridors of the Denver Tech Center to the historic bungalows of Washington Park and the new developments springing up in RiNo and Five Points. If you've called a plumber recently and been surprised by the price, you're not imagining it — costs have risen meaningfully over the past few years.

Here's what Denver homeowners actually pay for common plumbing jobs in 2026.

## Denver Plumber Cost by Job Type

### Service Call / Diagnostic: $100 – $200

Most Denver plumbers charge a dispatch or service call fee that covers showing up and diagnosing the problem. This ranges from $100–$200 for the trip alone, and is typically applied toward the total if you proceed with the repair.

After-hours and emergency calls carry surcharges — expect to pay $150–$250 extra for evenings, weekends, or holidays.

### Drain Clearing: $150 – $350

Clogged drains are the most common plumbing service call. Rates vary by location and severity:

- **Kitchen or bathroom drain snake**: $150–$250
- **Main sewer line clearing**: $200–$400
- **Hydro-jetting (heavy grease or root buildup)**: $400–$700

Denver's older neighborhoods (Washington Park, Curtis Park, Berkeley) have aging sewer infrastructure, and root intrusion is a recurring issue. If you're dealing with frequent backups, a camera inspection ($200–$400) is worth it.

### Water Heater Replacement: $900 – $1,800

Denver's hard water (the Front Range is notoriously high in mineral content) accelerates water heater degradation. Most tank heaters in Denver fail within 8–10 years due to scale buildup. Replacement costs:

- **40-gal gas water heater (standard)**: $900–$1,300 installed
- **50-gal gas water heater**: $1,000–$1,600 installed
- **Tankless gas water heater**: $1,800–$4,000 installed
- **Heat pump water heater (electric)**: $1,500–$3,000 installed

> **Pro Tip:** Denver's high altitude (5,280+ feet) affects water heater performance. Gas water heaters may need to be derated for altitude — make sure your plumber accounts for this when sizing and installing your new unit. An undersized or improperly calibrated water heater at altitude will underperform.

### Pipe Repair and Replacement: $300 – $5,000+

Denver's older housing stock means pipe issues are common. Key factors affecting cost:

- **Exposed supply line repair**: $300–$600
- **Pipe repair in wall or floor (with access)**: $500–$1,500
- **Full house repipe (galvanized to PEX)**: $3,500–$8,000

Many Denver homes built before 1970 still have galvanized steel pipes. These corrode from the inside over time, causing low water pressure, rust-colored water, and eventually leaks. If your Denver home has original galvanized pipes, budget for repiping sooner rather than later.

### Freeze-Related Pipe Damage: $200 – $3,000+

Denver winters are no joke. Temperatures regularly drop below 0°F, and improperly insulated pipes in crawl spaces, garages, and exterior walls are vulnerable. Freeze-related repairs:

- **Thawing frozen pipes**: $150–$300 (if no burst)
- **Burst pipe repair (single location)**: $300–$800
- **Extensive freeze damage (multiple bursts)**: $2,000–$8,000+

Prevention is far cheaper: foam pipe insulation runs $1–$3 per linear foot and can save a catastrophic repair bill.

## What Denver Plumbers Charge Per Hour

Most Denver plumbers bill in one of two ways:
- **Flat-rate pricing**: Fixed price per job, regardless of time (more predictable)
- **Time and materials**: Hourly rate plus parts

Denver plumber hourly rates typically run **$95–$165/hr** for standard work. Master plumbers and specialty work (gas lines, repiping, backflow testing) commands the higher end. This is roughly 10–20% above the national average, reflecting Denver's tight labor market.

## Licensing in Colorado

Colorado requires plumbers to hold a state license through the Colorado Division of Professions and Occupations. You can verify any plumber's license at dpo.colorado.gov. Always check before hiring — unlicensed plumbing work won't pass inspection and can create liability issues at resale.
