# How Much Does It Cost to Build a Deck? (2025 Pricing Guide)

A well-built deck adds living space, boosts curb appeal, and consistently delivers strong ROI at resale. But before you start dreaming about summer cookouts, you need a realistic sense of what you're about to spend.

The average deck build in 2025 runs between **$15,000 and $35,000** for a 400 sqft ground-level deck, depending heavily on material choice and design complexity. Here's exactly what drives that number.

## Deck Cost by Material

### Pressure-Treated Wood: $15–$35 per Square Foot

Pressure-treated (PT) lumber is the most budget-friendly option and the most common. It handles moisture and insects well when properly maintained, and it's widely available everywhere.

- **Total installed cost**: $6,000–$14,000 for a 400 sqft deck
- **Lifespan**: 15–25 years with regular maintenance (staining/sealing every 2–3 years)
- **Pros**: Lowest upfront cost, easy to work with, widely available
- **Cons**: Requires ongoing maintenance, can splinter, may warp over time

PT lumber prices have stabilized since the post-pandemic lumber spike, but they're still higher than pre-2020 levels. Budget accordingly.

### Composite Decking: $25–$55 per Square Foot

Composite is made from a mix of wood fiber and recycled plastic. It looks great, requires minimal maintenance, and has become increasingly popular as prices have come down.

- **Total installed cost**: $10,000–$22,000 for a 400 sqft deck
- **Popular brands**: Trex, TimberTech, Fiberon, Azek
- **Lifespan**: 25–30 years with minimal maintenance
- **Pros**: No staining or sealing required, resists moisture and insects, consistent appearance
- **Cons**: Higher upfront cost, can get hot in direct sun, can't be refinished

Trex and TimberTech are the category leaders. Mid-tier composite (Trex Enhance, TimberTech Prime+) runs $30–$40/sqft installed and is the sweet spot for most homeowners.

### Cedar and Redwood: $20–$45 per Square Foot

Cedar and redwood are naturally rot-resistant and beautiful. They're a step up from PT in aesthetics and natural durability, though still require maintenance.

- **Total installed cost**: $8,000–$18,000 for a 400 sqft deck
- **Lifespan**: 20–30 years with proper care
- **Pros**: Natural beauty, natural insect and rot resistance, holds finishes well
- **Cons**: More expensive than PT, availability varies by region, still needs periodic sealing

Cedar is most common in the Pacific Northwest and Northern states. In other regions, PT lumber may be more readily available and cost less.

## What Else Affects Deck Cost

### Size and Layout

Deck contractors price primarily by square footage, but layout complexity matters too:

- Simple rectangular deck: baseline price
- L-shaped or multi-level deck: add 15–30%
- Curved or custom cutouts: add 20–40%

A 200 sqft deck costs more per sqft than a 600 sqft deck — fixed costs (permits, site prep, hardware) are spread over fewer boards.

### Railings

Railings are required by code on any deck more than 30 inches above grade, and they're a significant line item:

- **Pressure-treated wood railings**: $15–$25 per linear foot
- **Composite railings**: $25–$45 per linear foot
- **Aluminum balusters with composite rail**: $35–$60 per linear foot
- **Cable railing**: $60–$100+ per linear foot

For a 200 sqft deck with 40 linear feet of railing, expect to add $600–$4,000 depending on material.

### Stairs

Each step of deck stairs runs $100–$200 in materials and labor. A standard 4-step stair section costs $400–$800 installed. Grand or curved stairs can run $1,500–$5,000+.

### Permits

Most jurisdictions require a permit for any deck over 200 sqft or over 30" above grade. Permit costs vary:

- Rural/suburban counties: $100–$400
- Urban/suburban cities: $300–$1,500

Don't skip permits. An unpermitted deck can complicate your home sale, void your homeowner's insurance, and require expensive teardown if discovered.

### Add-Ons That Inflate the Budget

- **Pergola or shade structure**: $3,000–$10,000
- **Built-in bench seating**: $500–$2,000
- **Deck lighting**: $500–$3,000
- **Privacy screens**: $1,000–$5,000
- **Hot tub framing/reinforcement**: $500–$2,000 extra

> **Pro Tip:** If you're on the fence between PT lumber and composite, run the 10-year math. PT requires staining/sealing every 2–3 years at $300–$600 per session. Over 10 years that's $1,200–$2,400 in maintenance. Composite has essentially zero maintenance cost. The gap narrows significantly on a 10-year basis — especially if you value your weekends.

## DIY vs. Hiring a Contractor

A handy homeowner can frame and board a simple ground-level deck, potentially saving $5,000–$8,000 in labor. But consider:

- You still need a permit and inspection
- Structural framing errors are expensive to fix after the fact
- Multi-level or elevated decks require precise engineering
- Composite installation has specific manufacturer requirements that affect warranty

The safest approach: hire out the framing and structural work, and do the decking boards yourself if you're comfortable. Many contractors will accommodate this split.

## Typical Deck Project Timeline

- Permit approval: 2–6 weeks (varies widely by jurisdiction)
- Construction: 3–7 days for a simple deck, 1–3 weeks for complex builds

Order materials ahead of time — lumber and composite can have lead times, especially on specialty colors.
